✦ What is Vedic Astrology? ▼ more

Astrology is a system built on the idea that the sky at the exact moment of your birth carries meaningful information about who you are and the life you came here to live. Astrologers map planet positions against the zodiac — a symbolic wheel of 12 signs — and interpret how those positions shape personality, relationships, timing, and soul direction.

Vedic astrology (Jyotish) is the system used in this planner. It's the traditional astrology of India, thousands of years old. It uses the sidereal zodiac — anchored to the actual positions of the fixed stars in the sky. This is different from Western astrology, which uses the tropical zodiac, anchored to the seasons.

The two systems use the same planet names and sign names, but their calculations differ by about 24° (called the Ayanamsha). This is why your Vedic Rising sign (Gemini) differs from your Western Rising sign (Cancer) — same sky, different measurement system. Neither is wrong. They illuminate different layers of the same person.

This planner uses Lahiri Ayanamsha — the most widely used Vedic calculation, recognized by the Indian government — and Whole Sign Houses, the oldest house system in Vedic astrology.

✦ In your planner: All planet positions in the sidebar are sidereal (Vedic). The Western Big Three section shows your tropical positions for comparison. The lunation dates (new moons, full moons) use tropical sign names but Vedic house assignments from your Gemini Rising.
↑ Rising Sign · The Lagna ▼ more

The Rising sign (Lagna) is the zodiac sign that was on the eastern horizon at the exact moment and place you were born. Because the Earth rotates, a new sign rises every ~2 hours — which is why birth time matters so much. It's not your "personality" the way a Sun sign is. It's more like the body and lens your soul chose for this lifetime. Everything in the chart is colored by it.

In Vedic astrology, the Rising sign is considered the most important factor in the chart — even more important than the Sun sign. It determines your house system (which areas of life each sign governs), your chart ruler (which planet runs the show), and your overall physical constitution and approach to life.

✺ Nakshatras — The 27 Lunar Mansions ▼ more

Nakshatras are 27 divisions of the zodiac, each spanning 13°20'. They're like a zoom lens inside each sign — more precise than just knowing your planet's sign. Each nakshatra has its own ruling planet, deity, symbol, and set of qualities.

They originated in ancient India as the Moon's "resting places" — the 27 nights it takes for the Moon to travel around the zodiac. Each night the Moon rests in a different nakshatra, which is why nakshatras are especially important for emotional, instinctive, and timing-based readings.

In this planner, every planet's nakshatra is listed next to its sign. The Rising nakshatra is particularly significant — it adds a specific quality and story to your Rising sign's energy.

⧗ Dashas — The Timing System ▼ more

The Vimshottari Dasha system is one of Vedic astrology's most distinctive features — a 120-year cycle of planetary periods that determine which planet governs your life during a given span of years. Unlike transits (where you wait for a planet to visit), the dasha system runs on an internal clock set by the Moon's nakshatra at your birth.

There are major periods (Mahadashas) that last many years (the Sun's period is 6 years; Jupiter's is 16 years; Saturn's is 19 years), and within each major period there are shorter sub-periods (Antardashas) that each run for months to a couple of years.

The planet running your dasha acts like the CEO of your life during that time — its nature, house, and condition in your chart shape everything that period brings. A well-placed planet running a dasha brings ease, recognition, and growth in its domains. A challenged planet brings lessons.

☊ Rahu & Ketu — The Nodes ▼ more

Rahu (North Node) and Ketu (South Node) are shadow planets — they are not physical bodies but the two points where the Moon's orbit intersects the Sun's path. They are always exactly opposite each other in the chart and are always retrograde (moving backward through the zodiac).

Ketu represents where you've already been — past-life mastery, gifts that feel innate, things that come easily but may feel hollow or uninteresting because they're already known. Ketu placements often describe skills and wisdom that feel "already inside you."

Rahu represents where you're going — the evolutionary edge of this lifetime, what you're here to develop and experience that you haven't mastered before. Rahu placements feel urgent, magnetizing, slightly overwhelming. They describe what pulls you forward even when it's uncomfortable.

♃ Transits — Planets Moving Through Your Chart ▼ more

Your birth chart is fixed — it's a snapshot of where the planets were when you were born. But the planets keep moving. Transits are the current positions of the planets in the sky, and when a transiting planet passes through a particular house in your chart, it activates the themes of that house.

Slower planets (Jupiter, Saturn, Rahu/Ketu) have the biggest impact because they spend months or years in each sign. Faster planets (Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars) move quickly and bring shorter, more subtle activations.
