Chapter Six
The Timing System · How the Chart Knows When

Western astrology uses transits — where planets are right now — to time events. Vedic astrology adds a second system called the Vimshottari Dasha: a 120-year cycle of planetary periods, each planet governing your life for a set number of years in turn. Right now, you are in the Jupiter Mahadasha — Jupiter's period runs from 2013 to 2029. 16 years of Jupiter governing your life chapter.

Jupiter, as we established, is exalted in your 10th house of career. This means your entire professional arc since age 9 has been under Jupiter's blessing — even in the years that didn't look like it yet. The seeds were being planted. You are now in the harvest.

Within Jupiter's 16-year period, there are sub-periods (antardashas) — shorter chapters governed by each planet in sequence. Right now you're in Jupiter–Mars antardasha: Jupiter's expansion operating through Mars's energy of physical action, discipline, and independent achievement. Mars rules your 2nd house (money/values) and 7th house (Rahu, partnerships). This sub-period is specifically about earning through physical, disciplined work and laying the foundation for the partnerships that will accelerate your next chapter. The crane is Jupiter–Mars made literal.

In November 2026, the sub-period shifts to Jupiter–Rahu. Rahu in your 7th house. Jupiter expanding Rahu's unconventional, ambitious energy. This runs until February 2029 — over two years of your greatest growth coming through extraordinary, catalyzing one-on-one partnerships and unconventional expansion. If something or someone arrives in late 2026 or 2027 that feels fated, fast, and accelerating — that is Jupiter–Rahu doing exactly what it was designed to do.

The whole planner year in one view: June–July: Jupiter in 10th (career peak, crane). August: Jupiter into 11th, birthday (income opens). September: New Moon in 1st (identity reset). November: Jupiter–Rahu begins (partnership era starts). 2027: A new moon in every house, one by one, the chart walking you through every area of your life. By July 2027, when the new moon returns to Cancer (your 10th house), you will be a different person standing in the same career. The chart is not static. It is a living document. Chapter Seven · The Bridge
Two Systems, One You — How Vedic and Western Layer

You have two charts: a Vedic (sidereal) chart and a Western (tropical) chart. They use different starting points for the zodiac — the Vedic system is fixed to the actual stars (corrected by ~24°), while the Western system is fixed to the seasons. Neither is wrong. They show different layers of the same person.

Your Vedic chart: Libra Rising, Cancer 10th, Moon in 1st. Shows your spiritual purpose, your karma, the deep structural story of your soul's work in this life.
Your Western chart: Scorpio Rising, Leo Sun, Scorpio Moon. Shows your psychological landscape, your personality expression, how you navigate the visible human world.

The interesting thing: both systems agree on the aesthetic Andie. Vedic: Libra Rising (beauty, value, Venus) + Vishakha (fire, focus, achievement) + Scorpio Rising in the West (magnetism, intensity, depth). Western: Leo Sun (bold, golden, wants to be seen) + Scorpio Moon (dark, deep, hides what it feels most). Both layers tell the same story: bold on the surface, vast on the inside. The dark reds and greens and black you chose are your psychological truth, not just an aesthetic preference.

The Vedic chart governs the timing system (dashas, nakshatras) and is more precise about career and life structure. The Western chart is excellent for understanding emotional patterns, relationships, and psychological themes. Use them both. They are lenses, not competing truths.

You are Libra Rising learning to need no mirror to know she's whole. You are Scorpio Moon learning that depth is power, not burden. You are Vishakha Rising: the archer who keeps aiming. You are Jupiter exalted in the 10th: the professional whose work matters and will be remembered. All of this is already in you. This planner just helps you watch it unfold.
